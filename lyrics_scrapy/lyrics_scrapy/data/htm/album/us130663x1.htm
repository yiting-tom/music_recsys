Album Intro：

Living Era’s 'The Last of the Red Hot Mamas' (As She was Known) Presents a Matchless Survey of Sophie Tucker’s Most Famous Recordings, Opening with the Million-seller She Made with Ted Lewis in 1926, Some of These Days. Her Four Biggest Pre-electric Hits Are Included (You’ve Got to See Mama Every Night . . .), As is the Great Quartet of 1927 Tracks Backed by Miff Mole and his Molers (After You’ve Gone . . .). In all There Are 14 Chart Hits (There’ll Be Some Changes Made, the Lady is a Tramp . . .) Including Another Million-seller in her Heartfelt My Yiddishe Momme. She was the Mistress of the Double Entendre (Just Listen to He Hadn’t Up ’til Yesterday), and She Provided the Theme for a Mature Generation in Life Begins at Forty. In Every Sense, a Great Star!
