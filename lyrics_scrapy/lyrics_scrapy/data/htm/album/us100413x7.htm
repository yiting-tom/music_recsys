Album Intro：

The wider pop audience, who thinks that Lene Marlin returns with the contagious pop songs 'Sitting Down Here' and 'Unforgivable Sinner', will have to be waiting in vain.

With 'Twist The Truth' Lene Marlin underscores that her native expression is belonging to melancholic pop related to inter-human problems and the everlasting theme of love.

Even 'Magnet' Johansen and Børge Fjordheim give 'Twist The Truth' an airy and open production which suits Lene Marlin well. Her voice is positioned in front, above, and in the centre, well supported by the classical guitar and simple arrangements with delicious details.

Song-wise, 'Here We Are' is well-known for the radio audience, and the song is giving a good indication about how the rest of the album sounds.

The audience that has gotten used to the grown-up, a bit melancholic, Lene Marlin is getting exactly what they would expect on 'Twist The Truth'.
