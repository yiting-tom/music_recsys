Album Intro：

The musical virtuoso is back with his best album yet; Standing Ovation shows Richie has risen to the occasion and is making social commentary about realities of life, this is reflected in tracks such as 'Cut It Off' and 'Jungle' (featuring Hanour). The consummate romantic and irresistible lover-boy, Richie has 'Can't Tek Mi Girl', 'Standing Ovation', 'Quiet Storm' and 'Take Me Away' in his arsenal of Lovers Rock, defying anyone to challenge his lyrical genius and passionate appeal. 'Life Without Music' and 'Where Is The Love' (featuring Cocoa Tea are a tribute to the value of music and its necessity as part of life. 'Fire Tonight' featuring General Degree is a Latin influenced track which adds flavor and diversity to this album. 'Gone Too Soon' is Richie's tribute to Dennis where he uniquely uses a medley of Dennis's song to honour him. 'Mama's Blessing' shows Richie's adoration of his mother and their special mother and son relationship, here his mother Carmen joins him on this track; a fitting climax to this remarkable compilation of well-produced authentic reggae tracks.
