Album Intro：

Monkey Minds In The Devil's Time is the second album from Steve Mason. The title refers to the Buddhist term for the easily distracted brain. The album features 20 songs: 9 recorded in London with producer Dan Carey (Hot Chip, The Kills, Django Django) and 11 short linking pieces self-produced in Steve's studio. The songs reference and enhance the best of Steve's decade and a half recording career- with nods to the loose uplift of the Beta Band, the fleet-footed electronics of his first solo venture King Biscuit Time and then into the electric soul of his album Boys Outside.
