Album Intro：

Digitally remastered and expanded deluxe two CD edition of the Irish rockers' 1976 album. Johnny The Fox was Thin Lizzy's seventh studio album. The album was written and recorded while bassist/vocalist Phil Lynott was recovering from a bout of hepatitis that put him off the road halfway through the Jailbreak tour. As a Rock album, it stands with the best of Thin Lizzy's work. Released during the band's peak years (the mid- to late '70s), the album highlights the twin guitar attack of Scott Gorham and Brian Robertson and Lynott's own songwriting - the songs on offer revealing a more focused craftsmanship. The overall tone of the album is looser than previous albums. It contains the all time classic 'Don't Believe a Word' and other Lizzy staples. The deluxe version contains a BBC session from the time as well as previously unreleased tracks. The package is rounded off with new sleeve notes and has a booklet packed with rare photos and memorabilia
