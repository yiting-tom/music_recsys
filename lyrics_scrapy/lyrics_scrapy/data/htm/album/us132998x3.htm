Album Intro：

The highly anticipate, revolutionary new studio album from underground industrial icons Razed In Black!

Features the singles Share This Poison, Vision (featuring Athan Maroulis of Spahn Ranch) and Blush!

Special bonus disc with 8 remixes and 2 enhanced CDR videos of live performances!

This release is poised to redefine modern industrial/EBM music, incorporating trance and techno influences with amazing results!

Full-on, massive marketing and press onslaughts including posters, postcards and ads in Outburn, Alternative Press, Blue Divide, MK Ultra and many more!

Every once in a while an album comes along that completely redefines its genre, breaks the rules, cross-breeds the styles and opens the door to a whole new world of multi-layered musical soundscapes. Damaged, the brand new studio release from underground industrial icons Razed In Black, is one such album. Since 1996, Razed In Black have released a series of full-length albums and EPs, each one an emotionally charged mixture of throbbing electronic manipulations and heavily distorted guitars. The band promptly found a devoted following among those in the gothic/darkwave scene, who flocked in droves to RIB live performances and snatched up merchandise by the tons, making the band one of the most important underground acts in the scene.

Now, a new era of RIB, and the industrial music genre in general, will begin. Damaged is simply one of the most intelligent hybrids of trance techno programming, darkwave songwriting and brutally aggressive guitar riffs. Think Paul Oakenfold meets NIN meets Ministry. Founder and leading creative force behind RIB, Romell Regulacion, has ample experience in the world of techno, spinning at clubs, releasing albums and contributing to the soundtrack of major films such as Carrie 2: The Rage and Mystery Men under the moniker Transmutator. This new album brings these two strands, techno and industrial, and threads them together with expertise and finesse to create one of the most densely-layered, seductively dark yet wholly invigorating albums to come along in quite some time. --Cleopatra Records
