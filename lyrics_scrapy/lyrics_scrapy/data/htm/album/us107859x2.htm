Album Intro：

After getting her start on the Hannah Montana show, actress Emily Osment became the latest in a long line of Disney starlets to launch a music career. She made her debut with 2009』s All the Right Wrongs, a spunky EP featuring Avril-influenced chick rock and distribution by the hard rock label Wind-Up Records. Osment remains with Wind-Up for her full-length debut, but she switches gears considerably, embracing electronic dance-pop and club culture instead. Out with the guitars; in with the synthesizers. Out with the punky lyrics; in with the double entendres. Fight or Flight often sounds like Katy Perry with a sneer, with a sexual undercurrent bubbling close to the surface and production from the same guy who guided Gwen Stefani's solo career. ~ Andrew Leahey, Rovi.
