Album Intro：

Limited digitally remastered and expanded edition of this 1987 album from The Dukes Of Stratosphear, the '60s-obsessed alter-ego of XTC, packaged in a hardback digibook featuring brand new artwork, sleevenotes, five previously unreleased demos and the enhanced video for 'You're A Good Man Albert Brown'. The Dukes Of Stratosphear was almost the worlds worst secret: everyone knew it was XTC, but that did not seem to matter. After the runaway success of their 1985 mini-album 25 O clock this was the Dukes' first album proper. Produced by John Leckie in Sawmills Studio in Cornwall, the album is littered with influences from the 60 s. In fact, if you didn't know it beforehand, you'd swear that this album was recorded during the highs of the late '60s Psyche movement! 2009.

Five unissued demos and the You're a Good Man Albert Brown video join Vanishing Girl; You're My Drug , and the rest 16 tracks!
