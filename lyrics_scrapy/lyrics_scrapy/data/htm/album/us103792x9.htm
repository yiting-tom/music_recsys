Album Intro：

1991's Mondo Bizarro is the Ramones' first studio album after the departure of founding bassist Dee Dee Ramone who is replaced here by Christopher Joseph Ward aka C.J. Ramone. However, Dee Dee's songwriting contributions still remain in the form of handful of tracks including 'Poison Heart' and 'Strength to Endure.' Producer/engineer Ed Stasium, who worked with the Ramone's on their albums Leave Home, Rocket To Russia and Road To Ruin, also makes his welcome return here, ultimately helping the band produce some of the strongest material of their final years. 180g colored vinyl pressing from Let Them Eat Vinyl! Established in 2004, Let Them Eat Vinyl specializes in vinyl editions of classic albums and is dedicated to providing top quality releases for record collectors and music fans worldwide. Their releases are pressed on exquisite, heavy weight vinyl to produce the very best in sound quality while beautiful vinyl colors are carefully chosen to complement the artwork, with some releases available in a variety of colors and configurations.
