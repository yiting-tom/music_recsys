Album Intro：

Pop-rock hit-makers Tommy James & The Shondells had a short but
extraordinary run, scoring 14 Top 40 Billboard® pop singles from 1966-1969.
Their high-spirited sound remains one of the most distinctive hallmarks of the
era and an essential part of the classic rock songbook. Formed in Dayton,
Ohio, the group had breakout success with their debut Roulette Records
single of the Jeff Barry/Ellie Greenwich tune
Hanky Panky, which shot to #1. It opens this
essential single-disc set, with other highlights
including the also chart-topping Crimson And
Clover, the Top 5 smashes Mony Mony,
Crystal Blue Persuasion, and Draggin
The Line, the latter one of Tommy James
70s-era solo hits.
