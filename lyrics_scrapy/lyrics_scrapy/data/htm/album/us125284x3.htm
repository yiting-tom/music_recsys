Album Intro：

With The Rural Alberta Advantage's album, Departing, the band further refines the exuberant guitar work; everything-on-the-table singing; songwriting full of conviction and detail; and majestic, keyboard-sprinkled arrangements that have won it so many fans. Departing strings together themes of small towns, Canadian fall and winter, breakup, and redemption and serves as a companion piece to their beloved debut album Hometowns. Highlights include the affecting 'North Star,' the stark regret of 'Tornado,' and the storming, percussive surge of 'Stamp,' all of which vividly set the scene.
