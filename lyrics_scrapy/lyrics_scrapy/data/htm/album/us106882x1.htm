Album Intro：

Two Suns is the second album by English solo artist Bat for Lashes (pseudonym of Natasha Khan), due to be released in April 6, 2009.

The album features collaborations with Scott Walker and Yeasayer and is the follow-up to Mercury Music Prize nominated album Fur and Gold.

Acording to the press release, 'Two Suns' is 'a record of modern-day fables exploring dualities on a number of levels – two lovers, two planets, two sides of a personality,' bringing reflection about 'the philosophy of the self and duality, examining the need for both chaos and balance, for both love and pain, in addition to touching on metaphysical ideas concerning the connections between all existence.'.

In 'Two Suns', Natasha Khan also presents an alter-ego, named 'Pearl', which was described by the press-release as 'a destructive, self-absorbed, blonde, femme fatale of a persona who acts as a direct foil to Khan's more mystical, desert-born spiritual self.'

The first single from the album, released in March 1, 2009, is 'Daniel'. Two Suns was recorded in segments in California, New York, London, Brighton and Wales.

It was co-produced by Khan and Dave Kosten, her collaborator on Fur and Gold.
